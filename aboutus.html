FPCI Chapter UIN Jakarta

Introduction to FPCI Chapter UIN Jakarta

FPCI Chapter UIN Jakarta, established in 2020, is a student community based in UIN Jakarta as a university-level chapter of FPCI with direct subordinate relation to FPCI. Therefore, FPCI Chapter UIN Jakarta shares the same goals as FPCI, which is to spread Indonesia internationalism. FPCI Chapter UIN Jakarta in the Board of 25.26 brings the vision with a reference to the concept of “Syi’ar” in Islam, which emphasizes the visible expression and dissemination of faith, values, and identity. Drawing from this concept, FPCI UIN Jakarta aspires to become a beacon that radiates its unique character—rooted in Islamic values and local wisdom—into the global arena. By integrating knowledge, values, and identity, FPCI UIN Jakarta seeks to foster a form of internationalism that is both intellectually grounded and culturally resonant. Through programs such as Foreign Policy Talks (FOLKS), Social Culture Opinion among People (SCOOP), Diplomatic Academy, and others, FPCI Chapter UIN Jakarta seeks to actively engage diverse audiences in international discourse, while embodying the spirit of syi’ar—conveying values not only through words, but through impactful presence and action.

What is FPCI?

Foreign Policy Community of Indonesia (FPCI) is an independent, non-partisan organization committed to fostering Indonesian internationalism by engaging diplomats, government officials, academia, businesses, and students in discussions on critical international relations.
